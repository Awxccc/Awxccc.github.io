Macronutrients

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the main energy source for your body and are found in various foods like fruits, vegetables, grains, and dairy products.
Carbohydrates are classified into sugars, starches, and fiber, each playing a unique role in providing energy and supporting bodily functions.

Sugars

Simple carbs that are quickly digested. Found in fruits, sweets, and soft drinks.

Starches

Complex carbs found in rice, potatoes, and bread. Provide sustained energy.

Fibers

Indigestible carbs that aid digestion. Found in veggies, whole grains, and legumes.

Proteins

Proteins are essential for the body's growth, repair, and overall function.
They are split into 3 categories, complete, incomplete and complementary proteins.
Composed of amino acids, the building blocks the body uses to create new proteins.

Complete Proteins

Contain all essential amino acids. Found in meat, dairy, eggs, and soy.

Incomplete Proteins

Lack one or more essential amino acids. Found in beans, nuts, and grains.

Complementary Proteins

Combining incomplete proteins to make a complete one.

Fats

Fats are involved in production of certain hormes and also provide energy, help absorb vitamins for daily activites and bodily functions.
Fats serve as an energy storage as they are stored in the body as adipose tissue, providing a reserve of energy for later use.
Helps to maintain body temperature by providing insulation against cold.

Unsaturated Fats

Healthy fats that help reduce cholesterol. Found in olive oil, avocados, and fish.

Saturated Fats

Consumption should be limited. Found in butter, cheese, and red meat.

Trans Fats

Unhealthy fats linked to heart disease. Found in processed foods and snacks.

Micronutrients

Micronutrients are essential vitamins and minerals needed by the body in small amounts for optimal health, growth, and development.
They play crucial roles in various bodily functions, including enzyme production, hormone regulation, and cellular processes.
Deficiencies in micronutrients can lead to a range of health problems, some of which can be severe. There are 4 types: Water-soluble vitamins, Fat-soluble vitamins, Macrominerals, and Trace minerals.

Dietary Guidelines

Follow official guidelines to maintain a healthy weight, stay active, and prevent chronic diseases.
